Background
Understanding salary dynamics across various job roles, industries, and geographic regions is essential in today’s competitive job market. Job candidates rely on accurate compensation benchmarks to make informed career decisions, while HR professionals use these insights to design attractive and equitable pay structures. Traditional salary estimation methods often fall short due to the complexity introduced by factors such as company size, industry-specific pay norms, regional cost-of-living differences, employer reputation, and job market trends. 


Objectives

To address these limitations, this project applies machine learning—specifically regression modeling—to predict salary outcomes based on key input variables such as job title, location, and company attributes. Regression modeling is particularly effective for predicting continuous values like salary ranges by identifying patterns in large datasets. This data-driven approach enhances accuracy in salary predictions, offers strategic insights for talent acquisition, and improves decision-making for both HR departments and job seekers.


Problem Statement 

Low-rated states offer salaries that average 23.5% below the national benchmark, creating a significant pay gap that reduces their competitiveness in attracting and retaining top data science talent. This disparity limits the ability of companies in these regions to hire skilled professionals, potentially impacting innovation, productivity, and long-term organizational growth. A data-driven approach is needed to align regional salary structures with market expectations and improve talent acquisition outcomes.
